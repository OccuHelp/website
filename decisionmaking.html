Our Decision-Making Framework

At OccuHelp, we believe that how we make decisions is just as important as the decisions themselves. Our structured approach ensures that we consistently make choices that align with our values and serve our clients' best interests.

Define the Problem

We start by clearly articulating the problem or opportunity, ensuring we understand its scope and impact. This includes gathering relevant data and identifying stakeholders who may be affected.

Key questions we ask:

  • What is the core issue we're trying to address?
  • Who is affected by this decision?
  • What data do we need to understand the situation fully?

Align with Values and Mission

Every decision we make is evaluated against our core values and mission. This ensures that our actions consistently reflect what we stand for as an organization.

We consider:

  • How does this decision support our mission of improving workplace safety?
  • Which of our core values are relevant to this decision?
  • Are there any potential conflicts with our values?

Generate and Evaluate Options

We develop multiple potential solutions and evaluate them based on their alignment with our values, potential impact, feasibility, and risks.

Our evaluation criteria include:

  • Effectiveness in addressing the core problem
  • Resource requirements and constraints
  • Potential risks and how they can be mitigated
  • Short and long-term implications

Make the Decision

After thorough evaluation, we select the option that best addresses the problem while aligning with our values and mission. We document the rationale behind our decision to ensure transparency.

Implement and Monitor

We develop a clear implementation plan with defined responsibilities and timelines. Throughout implementation, we monitor progress and outcomes, making adjustments as needed.

Key implementation considerations:

  • Who needs to be involved in implementation?
  • What metrics will we use to measure success?
  • How will we communicate the decision to stakeholders?

Review and Learn

We regularly review the outcomes of our decisions to evaluate their effectiveness and identify lessons learned. This continuous learning approach helps us refine our decision-making process over time.

Our review process includes:

  • Did we achieve the intended outcomes?
  • What went well and what could have been improved?
  • What can we learn for future decisions?

Our Framework in Action: Product Feature Prioritization

When deciding which features to include in our software updates, we apply our decision-making framework to ensure we're delivering the most value to our clients:

  1. Define: We gather user feedback and usage data to identify pain points and opportunities.
  2. Align: We evaluate potential features against our mission of improving workplace safety outcomes.
  3. Evaluate: We assess each feature's potential impact, development complexity, and alignment with our product roadmap.
  4. Decide: We prioritize features that deliver the greatest safety impact while being feasible within our resource constraints.
  5. Implement: Our development team builds the features with regular check-ins to ensure they're meeting the intended goals.
  6. Review: After release, we measure user adoption and impact on safety metrics to evaluate effectiveness.

Decision Principles

Data-Informed

We base our decisions on relevant data and evidence rather than assumptions or opinions alone.

Transparent

We clearly communicate the reasoning behind our decisions to build trust with stakeholders.

Inclusive

We involve relevant stakeholders in the decision-making process to ensure diverse perspectives are considered.

Long-Term Focused

We consider both immediate outcomes and long-term implications when making decisions.

  • Cost-Benefit Analysis
  • Risk Assessment Models
  • Stakeholder Impact Analysis
  • Scenario Planning
